# Persona Templates — Research & Sketch

**Status:** Phase 1 research deliverable. Sketches only. Depth to be expanded on the 1-2 personas you choose to ship first in the Demo/Sandbox Mode build.

**Scope:** 10 personas spanning different life stages, family structures, and pressures. Includes the three you specifically requested (single mother, single father, co-parent) plus seven others drawn from common archetypes in the self-reflection / life-organizing app space.

---

## How these personas were chosen

I looked for archetypes where:
1. The "mental load" is real and heavy enough that a reflective, organizing tool would be felt as relief rather than one more thing to manage
2. The person is likely in active identity work — questioning, rebuilding, reinventing, or protecting something — the core emotional territory UnPuzzle Life serves
3. Demo versions would feel authentic to prospects in that archetype, not generic ([Mental Health America on single parents](https://mhanational.org/resources/mental-health-and-the-single-parent/), [Heloa on single-mother mental load](https://heloa.app/en/blog/parents/daily-life/single-mother-mental-load))
4. There's a distinct voice — the language they'd use about their own life is different enough from other personas that the template wouldn't feel interchangeable

Some archetypes common in other apps (pure habit-tracker user, pure meditation seeker) were excluded — they'd flatten into a generic template that doesn't show UnPuzzle Life at its best.

## The 10 personas (sketch)

### 1. The Single Mother

**One-line positioning:** "Carrying it all, and trying to still be a whole person underneath it."

**Who she is:** 30s-40s, one or more kids, primary or sole caregiver. Works (full-time, part-time, or gig) and runs the household. Ex may or may not be involved. The mental load never stops — she's the only one who remembers the field trip, the co-pay, the permission slip, the pediatrician visit, the dentist, the dinner plan ([Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/resources/mental-health-and-the-single-parent/), [Heloa](https://heloa.app/en/blog/parents/daily-life/single-mother-mental-load)).

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** She doesn't need another task tracker — she needs a place where she exists as something other than "the one who does everything." Clarity Portal, especially Me-Time as a real area she's allowed to protect, is the quiet revolution.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Family, Kids, Work, Health, Me-Time, Money, Friendships (+ room for a Faith or Growth piece)
- **Purpose draft:** *"To raise these kids well — and to still be me while I'm doing it."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Me-Time:* "30 minutes that are mine before anyone else needs me. Coffee, not guilt."
  - *Kids:* "They know they are loved. They see me choose them, and they see me choose myself too."
  - *Money:* "A cushion. Enough that a surprise car repair doesn't send me into a spiral."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "Sunday morning is slow. No work calls at bath time. My body gets 20 minutes before the inbox does."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Mix of school logistics, a work block, a protected 30 min for herself, one small friendship touch (text a friend back), and an evening wind-down with the kids.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** Entries that name the hard moments honestly ("I lost it at bedtime tonight. Tomorrow I'll do better.") alongside small wins ("Made the deadline AND was there for pickup.").

### 2. The Single Father

**One-line positioning:** "Showing up for his kids without a map, and figuring out who he is when he's not performing."

**Who he is:** 30s-50s, custodial or shared custody. Often navigating social expectations that don't quite fit (the "dad-as-babysitter" perception, the lack of a single-dad peer network the way single moms have). Quietly carrying the financial and emotional load. Less likely to have asked for help.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** A private, reflective space that doesn't ask him to perform strength. Immutable laws are a useful form factor for someone who thinks in commitments and rules — it feels masculine-coded without being macho.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Fatherhood, Work, Health, Finances, Growth, Friendships, Me-Time
- **Purpose draft:** *"To be the dad my kids actually need — and to keep building a life I'm proud of."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Fatherhood:* "Present, not just available. They know me, not just my schedule."
  - *Health:* "Strong enough to still be here for them in 30 years. Not a project — a standard."
  - *Friendships:* "Two or three real ones I actually call."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "Phone down when they're home. Lift 3x a week. Call one friend a week, even when I don't feel like it."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Early workout, work focus blocks, deliberate "with-kids" time (not just "around-kids" time), a few minutes of reflection at night.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** Short, direct. "Was distracted at dinner. Noticed. Put the phone in the drawer." "My son asked a real question. I didn't rush the answer."

### 3. The Co-Parent

**One-line positioning:** "Two households, one kid — and figuring out how to make that feel whole."

**Who they are:** Separated or divorced, sharing custody in some configuration (50/50, every other weekend, whatever the arrangement). Could be any gender. Navigating logistics, communication with the other parent, and the emotional weirdness of having the kid "go" to a place they don't control. Often also dating or remarried, so additional family dynamics in play.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** A space to hold the full picture — their kid-time, their alone-time, the relationship with the ex, maybe a new partner. Life has more compartments now and they don't always talk to each other. UnPuzzle Life lets each compartment be real without collapsing into another.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Kids (with-me + between-homes awareness), Co-Parenting, Work, Partner (if applicable), Health, Self, Finances
- **Purpose draft:** *"To give my kids two homes that love them — and to build a life that fits who I'm becoming."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Co-Parenting:* "Calm, respectful, kid-focused. We don't have to be friends; we have to be a team on this."
  - *Kids (with-me):* "When they're here, they're here. Fully. Not the TV, not the phone."
  - *Self:* "I'm not just 'so-and-so's ex' or 'so-and-so's parent.' I'm a whole person with my own life."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "No venting about the other parent in front of the kids. Handoff is calm. I use my alone-weeks, not waste them."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Rhythm changes by week depending on custody schedule — the template reflects both states ("kid-week" vs "solo-week") with different default agenda shapes.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "First solo-week I actually enjoyed — instead of counting days." "Got through the handoff without a knot in my stomach."

**Design note:** Co-Parent is the one persona where the template might benefit from *two* agenda modes (kid-week and solo-week). Worth a small design conversation if we ship this one.

### 4. The Wife & Mother & Business Owner (your original example)

**One-line positioning:** "Building a business, raising a family, and trying not to lose herself in either role."

**Who she is:** 30s-50s, married, kids at home, owns or runs a business (service, retail, online, consulting — not necessarily a VC-funded startup). The business is real — not a side hustle — but so is the family. She's the emotional center of the household AND the operational center of the business.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** She's spent years context-switching. The Pieces model lets her actually see that she has more than two modes. The Clarity Portal gives her permission to be *herself* for 10 minutes before she's needed by anyone again.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Marriage, Family, Business, Health, Faith (optional), Me-Time, Friendships, Money
- **Purpose draft:** *"To build a life and a business that honor my family, my faith, and the woman I'm becoming — without losing myself in either role."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Marriage:* "We still date. We still surprise each other. We're partners, not just logistics buddies."
  - *Business:* "A business that serves the life, not a life that serves the business."
  - *Me-Time:* "Not guilty. Not earned. Mine."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "Sunday is family. No work calls during dinner. My body gets 30 min before the inbox does."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Early routine, family handoff, business focus blocks, deliberate dinner-is-dinner protection, evening wind-down.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Said no to a client for a family dinner. Felt weird. Did it anyway." "Hired out the thing I keep procrastinating. Freed up 3 hours a week."

### 5. The Recent Grad

**One-line positioning:** "Asking who they are when they're not performing for a transcript."

**Who they are:** 22-27, recently finished college, starting a career (or still figuring out what one is), living solo or with roommates or back home. Identity is in flux — student was a role, now that role is gone ([Gaia Center on 20s identity](https://gaiacenter.co/blog/navigating-identity-in-your-20s), [Business Insider grad piece](https://www.businessinsider.com/recent-college-graduate-just-wants-stability-in-twenties-2025-8)). Comparing themselves constantly to peers on social media.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** The wizard's Visualization phase is a *perfect* fit for someone whose identity is up for grabs. UnPuzzle Life gives them a private place to articulate their own answer to "who am I and what am I building?" — a question school stops asking the moment they walk across the stage.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Career, Health, Friendships, Money, Growth, Home, Romance, Family-of-origin
- **Purpose draft:** *"To build the life I actually want — not the one I'm supposed to want."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Career:* "Work I'm proud to talk about, that pays me enough to live, that leaves room for the rest of my life."
  - *Home:* "A place that feels like mine. Plants, good light, a rhythm."
  - *Money:* "I know what I make, what I spend, what I save. No shame, no panic."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "No doom-scrolling before coffee. 2 real-life friendships touched per week. Sunday reset every Sunday."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Anchor routines (sleep, workout, meals), job-hunt or work-day blocks, a deliberate growth slot (reading, learning), one social connection.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Applied to 3 jobs, one I actually want." "Took myself to a new coffee shop. Liked it." "Comparison got loud today. Noticed."

### 6. The Empty Nester

**One-line positioning:** "Learning who she is when she's not someone's full-time mother."

**Who she is:** 45-60, kids have moved out or are about to, decades of identity wrapped up in caregiving ([Women Reinventing Midlife](https://womenreinventingmidlife.com/coping-with-empty-nest-syndrome-letting-go-and-finding-new-purpose/), [Jen Rulon on reclaiming identity](https://jenrulon.com/reclaiming-identity-midlife/)). Possibly still married, possibly recently divorced, possibly widowed. The house is quieter. The calendar opened up and she's not sure what to put in it.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** This is maybe the *purest* fit for the app — someone actively reinventing, actively needing a Visualization exercise, actively redefining what her "amazing life" looks like now. Clarity Portal hits especially hard here because the portal framing ("MY TIME") is exactly what she's been denying herself for 20 years.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Me (literal — named this, not "Self"), Marriage, Grown Kids, Health, Growth, Friendships, Faith, Money, Home
- **Purpose draft:** *"To build the next chapter of my life on purpose — not by accident, not by default."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Me:* "I know what I love again. I pursue it without asking permission."
  - *Grown Kids:* "Close, but not fused. I'm their mom, not their manager."
  - *Health:* "Strong, mobile, energetic — for the next 30 years, not just this summer."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "One thing I love, every day. I ask for what I need. I move my body."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** A rhythm built around *her* — morning movement, a creative or learning block, a social or volunteer element, reflective time.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Booked the pottery class I've been thinking about for 2 years." "Didn't call my son first. He called me. It was enough."

### 7. The Solo Founder

**One-line positioning:** "Building something real, on borrowed time, with no off switch."

**Who they are:** Any age, any gender, building a business (startup, agency, studio, SaaS, creator business). High-agency, high-stakes, high-stress. [87.7% of entrepreneurs struggle with at least one mental health issue; 34.4% face burnout](https://founderreports.com/entrepreneur-mental-health-statistics/). Identity often fused with the company's success ([Lume on founder emotions](https://www.join-lume.com/lume-blog/how-to-navigate-founder-emotions-and-maintain-work-life-balance)).

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** The Clarity Portal's ritual ("MY TIME") directly counters the always-on, always-work founder identity. Immutable laws are a forcing function for protecting the non-business pieces. The Pieces model makes "you are not just your startup" visible, daily.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Company, Health, Relationship (partner/spouse), Friendships, Finances, Growth, Me
- **Purpose draft:** *"To build something that matters — without sacrificing the life it's supposed to serve."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Company:* "A business I'd be proud to describe to my grandkids. Profitable, useful, sustainable."
  - *Health:* "A non-negotiable base. Not optimized, just unbroken."
  - *Me:* "I'm a person with interests outside this company. I have hobbies I don't monetize."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "No laptop after 9 PM. Weekends are weekends. Exercise is as important as the standup."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Early movement, 2-3 deep work blocks, one non-work appointment (lunch with a friend, a walk, a hobby), a hard stop in the evening.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Closed the laptop at 8:45. Resisted the urge to open it." "Said no to a 'quick call' on Saturday. The world didn't end."

### 8. The Career Climber

**One-line positioning:** "Doing the work, getting promoted, and wondering if they still remember who they are."

**Who they are:** Late 20s-40s, on a clear professional trajectory in a corporate or high-demand field (tech, finance, law, medicine, consulting). Not a founder — inside a system, moving up it. Often single or in a relationship that's taken a back seat to the career. The wins are real, but the ground under them feels thin.

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** The Pieces model helps them see that "Work" is one of many areas, not the totality. The wizard's Visualization, done honestly, often surfaces tensions between their professional identity and the rest of their life that they've been avoiding.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Career, Health, Relationship, Friendships, Growth, Me-Time, Money, Family-of-origin
- **Purpose draft:** *"To do work I'm proud of — and to be a person I'm proud of outside of it."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Career:* "Progression I'm genuinely proud of, not just hitting external milestones. I like the work, not just the outcome."
  - *Relationship:* "My partner doesn't come second to my calendar. We're building something together."
  - *Me-Time:* "Things I do just because I like them. No productivity angle."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "Calendar closes at 7. One non-work thing every week I'd miss if I didn't protect it."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Structured morning, work-day, deliberate evening transition, relationship or friendship touch, reflective wind-down.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Left the office at 6:30. Guilt was loud. Did it anyway." "Realized I haven't seen [friend] in 3 months. Texted them."

### 9. The Faith-Centered Builder

**One-line positioning:** "Building a life around a faith that comes first — everything else flows from it."

**Who they are:** Any age, any gender, deeply rooted in a religious or spiritual practice (Christian, Muslim, Jewish, Buddhist, etc.) that shapes every other area of their life. Not the "I go to church on Easter" version — the "my faith is the lens I see the rest through" version. Common profile in a lot of UnPuzzle Life's natural audience (based on the "Faith" Piece being a common custom area).

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** The Purpose phase and Clarity's "MY TIME" framing both resonate deeply with people who see self-care as stewardship, not indulgence. The custom Piece system means Faith can sit at the top — literally first in the wheel — without the app imposing a generic "Spirituality" label.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Faith (first), Family, Health, Work, Friendships, Growth, Service/Ministry, Money
- **Purpose draft:** *"To live a life that honors God / my faith, my family, and the work I've been given — in that order."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Faith:* "Daily practice that's mine. Prayer / study / quiet — not performative, not skipped."
  - *Service/Ministry:* "A way I contribute outside my own household. Regular, not sporadic."
  - *Family:* "Loved well, seen, prayed for by name."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "First 15 minutes of the day are not the phone. Sabbath is protected. Tithe is automatic."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Morning practice, work/school blocks, family touches, a small service or ministry element, evening reflection or scripture.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Didn't skip prayer even when I was running late. Felt the difference." "Said yes to serving Saturday morning. Glad I did."

### 10. The Rebuilder

**One-line positioning:** "Coming out the other side of something hard — and deciding who they want to be now."

**Who they are:** Any age, any gender, recently through a major life event — divorce, loss of a loved one, recovery from addiction, major illness, career collapse, or a deliberate walk-away from a life that wasn't theirs. They're not in crisis anymore, but they're not back to normal either — and "normal" is no longer the goal. The question is: *who am I now, and what do I want to build from here?*

**Why UnPuzzle Life fits:** The wizard's Purpose + Visualization phases are almost literally therapy-adjacent — they ask the same questions a good therapist or recovery program asks. Immutable laws offer structure for someone rebuilding default behaviors from scratch. Clarity Portal's ritual gives them a sacred daily moment.

**Sketched template content:**
- **Pieces:** Health (physical + mental), Relationships, Me, Work, Growth, Money, Faith or Program (if applicable), Creative/Joy
- **Purpose draft:** *"To become someone I'm proud of — starting from where I actually am, not where I wish I were."*
- **Vision highlights:**
  - *Me:* "I know myself. I don't lie to myself. I like being alone with myself."
  - *Health:* "Stable. Sleeping. Moving. Eating like I matter."
  - *Creative/Joy:* "One thing I do for no reason other than it makes me feel alive."
- **Immutable laws (sample):** "Sleep before midnight. One honest conversation a week. I don't go back to what I left."
- **Daily agenda vibe:** Heavy on routine and stability — anchor practices (sleep, eat, move), one growth element, one connection, reflective writing at night.
- **Sample reviewed entries:** "Six months out. I don't miss it." "Had a hard night. Didn't do the thing. Called [person] instead."

## Recommendations — which to lead with

If you wanted to ship just **one** template first, I'd lead with the **Wife & Mother & Business Owner** — you've already been thinking about it, you know the voice, and it's an audience you likely have direct access to (friends, clients, people who'd give you honest feedback on whether the template feels authentic).

If you wanted **two**, I'd add the **Single Mother**. Different enough that the contrast proves the template system works for real personas, not just "slight variations of one person." And the audience overlap (many single mothers are also business owners, many business-owner moms also periodically carry single-parent loads) means these two templates cover a surprisingly wide crowd.

If you wanted **three**, the **Empty Nester** completes a powerful trio: life-building (Business Owner), life-surviving (Single Mother), and life-reinventing (Empty Nester). Three distinct narrative shapes, three distinct voices, three different "why this app" angles.

## Open questions for you

When you're ready to move from sketch to full template, these are the design decisions each persona will require:

1. **Default custom Pieces** — some personas (Co-Parent's "kid-week vs solo-week" agenda, Rebuilder's "Program" Piece) might benefit from structural features the app doesn't have yet. Do we simplify to fit the current app, or note them as design extensions?
2. **Real voice vs aspirational voice** — should the template entries sound like the user *is* living this life, or like they *want* to live it? Leaning aspirational but honest (the "I lost it at bedtime" entry for Single Mother is critical — pure aspiration feels fake).
3. **Demographic specificity** — should templates name things like a spouse's name ("Jon is asleep") or keep it generic ("my partner is asleep")? Generic feels safer; named feels more alive.
4. **Sample reviewed entry count** — enough to populate the review page but not so many it feels overwhelming. I'd suggest ~7 days of entries per persona.
5. **Time horizon** — is this persona in "early days with UnPuzzle Life" (sparse data) or "been using for a while" (rich history)? For demos, "rich history" is more compelling but harder to author realistically. Recommend: pick one per persona based on what sells.
